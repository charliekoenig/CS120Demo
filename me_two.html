A Trip I Took Recently:

I went to London and Paris for Spring Break to visit my girlfriend who spent the semester abroad. This was my first time traveling out of the country and I was flying alone so it was intimidating, but I made it. We took the train to Paris which was expensive but so much easier than flying. Despite my three years of middle school French, I was completely lost in conversation. On top of the language barrier, my overwhelming desire to be liked by random strangers made simple tasks like ordering a sandwich a mentally exhausting experience.

My Favorite Restuarant: Paraiso Cocina Dominicana

The food is great but I love the owner, Johan, more than anything else. It's $6 for a bowl plus whatever special Johan made that day, usually empanadas. In highschool, I would walk here for lunch a few times a week and come after practice if he hadn't sold out of food yet. (No website so I linked the instagram)

My Favorite Food

My favorite sushi is anything salmon-based with soy sauce and wasabi. Last summer, when I worked remote, I would walk to an all-you-can-eat place for lunch which always had a weekday lunch special. The quality might not have been the highest but at least I could afford it.

I've tried to make my own sushi before but the rice didn't turn out well and my rolling left a lot of room for improvement.
